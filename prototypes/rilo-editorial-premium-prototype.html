Decision Narrative Why this direction feels materially different
Editorial finish

A UI concept that behaves more like a premium report than a generic web app.

The prototype rejects the usual recipe—no purple/blue gradients, no stacked cards inside cards, no glowing data widgets. It borrows from luxury hospitality, financial magazines, and printed investment briefs to feel slower, more composed, and more expensive.

Typography does the heavy lifting.

A serif display gives the product a point of view, while the sans layer keeps captions and labels quietly operational.

The canvas is warm, not clinical.

Paper tones and softened rules help financial content feel intentional instead of sterile.

The whole page reads top to bottom.

It is easier to imagine this in strategy reviews, brand presentations, or investor-facing discussions than a default admin shell.

Suitable next steps Prototype the remaining views in the same language.

Scenario comparison, module inputs, and breakeven views could all inherit the same editorial grid without touching the live app.

Constraint respected Main application files remain untouched.

This concept is isolated as a standalone HTML file, self-contained, and safe to open locally without changing the existing product build.
